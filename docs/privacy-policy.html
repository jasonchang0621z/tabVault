Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 5, 2026

TabVault ("the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that lets you save and restore tab workspaces. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the Extension collects, how it is used, and your choices.

1. Data We Store

TabVault saves workspace data — including tab URLs, titles, favicon URLs, and tab group metadata (name, color, collapsed state) — to your browser's local storage using the chrome.storage.local API. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to any external server.

2. Analytics

TabVault may use Google Analytics or a similar service to collect anonymous, aggregated usage data such as:

  • Extension version and browser language
  • Feature usage counts (e.g., number of workspaces saved or restored)
  • Error reports for debugging

This data does not include personally identifiable information (PII), URLs you visit, or the content of your workspaces. You can opt out of analytics by disabling tracking in the Extension's settings (when available).

3. Data We Do Not Collect

  • Personal information (name, email, address)
  • Browsing history beyond what you explicitly save as a workspace
  • Passwords, form data, or cookies
  • Any data from pages you visit

4. Permissions

The Extension requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • tabs — to read and create tabs when saving/restoring workspaces
  • tabGroups — to read and recreate tab groups with their colors and titles
  • storage — to persist workspace data locally
  • alarms — to schedule automatic backups (Pro feature)

5. Third-Party Services

  • LemonSqueezy — If you purchase a Pro license, your license key is validated through the LemonSqueezy API. Only the license key is transmitted; no personal data or workspace content is shared. See LemonSqueezy's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Analytics — If analytics are enabled, anonymized usage data may be processed by Google Analytics. See Google's Privacy Policy.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

All workspace data is stored locally in your browser. You can delete individual workspaces from within the Extension, or remove all data by uninstalling the Extension. No data is retained on external servers.

7. Children's Privacy

TabVault is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
